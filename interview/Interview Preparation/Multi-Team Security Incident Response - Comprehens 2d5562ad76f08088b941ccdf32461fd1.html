Introduction

Security incidents often require coordination across multiple teams including security, engineering, operations, legal, PR, and executive leadership. This guide covers incident command structures, communication strategies, and best practices for multi-team coordination during security incidents.

Key Principle: Clear roles, communication, and coordination are essential for effective incident response.

Incident Response Team Structure

Incident Command Structure

Roles:

  • Incident Commander: Overall coordination
  • Technical Lead: Technical decisions
  • Communication Lead: Stakeholder communication
  • Legal/Compliance Lead: Legal and compliance matters
  • Business Lead: Business impact and priorities

Team Composition

Teams Involved:

  • Security Team
  • Engineering Team
  • Operations/DevOps Team
  • Legal Team
  • PR/Communications Team
  • Executive Leadership
  • Customer Support (if needed)

Team Roles and Responsibilities

Security Team

Responsibilities:

  • Lead incident response
  • Threat analysis
  • Vulnerability assessment
  • Forensics and investigation
  • Security remediation guidance

Engineering Team

Responsibilities:

  • System access and analysis
  • Code review and vulnerability fixes
  • System changes and deployment
  • Technical remediation

Operations Team

Responsibilities:

  • Infrastructure access
  • System monitoring
  • Configuration changes
  • Deployment and rollback
  • Infrastructure remediation

Legal Team

Responsibilities:

  • Regulatory compliance
  • Data breach notifications
  • Contract review
  • External communications
  • Legal risk assessment

Communication Strategies

Communication Channels

Channels:

  • Incident response chat (Slack, Teams)
  • Conference calls/video calls
  • Status dashboard
  • Email for formal communication
  • External communication (PR)

Communication Principles

Principles:

  • Regular updates (every 1-2 hours during active incidents)
  • Clear and concise
  • Accurate information
  • Don't speculate
  • Appropriate detail level for audience

Stakeholder Communication

Audiences:

  • Internal Teams: Technical details, status updates
  • Executive Leadership: High-level status, business impact
  • Legal/Compliance: Regulatory requirements, notifications
  • PR/External: Controlled messaging, customer communication
  • Customers: Transparent communication when required

Coordination Processes

Incident Triage

Process:

  1. Initial assessment
  1. Team notification
  1. Role assignment
  1. Communication channel setup
  1. Initial status update

Status Updates

Update Frequency:

  • Active incidents: Every 1-2 hours
  • Escalated incidents: More frequent
  • Resolved incidents: Final summary

Update Content:

  • Current status
  • Actions taken
  • Next steps
  • Known issues
  • Timeline

Decision-Making Framework

Decision Authority

Levels:

  • Tactical: Incident Commander (technical decisions)
  • Operational: Business Lead (business impact decisions)
  • Strategic: Executive Leadership (high-impact decisions)

Escalation Procedures

Escalation Triggers:

  • High business impact
  • Legal/compliance concerns
  • Resource requirements
  • External communication needed
  • Strategic decisions required

Tools and Platforms

Incident Management

Tools:

  • Incident tracking system (Jira, ServiceNow)
  • Status dashboard
  • Documentation (Confluence, Wiki)
  • Communication platform (Slack, Teams)

Communication Tools

Tools:

  • Chat platform for real-time communication
  • Video conferencing for meetings
  • Email for formal communication
  • Status page for external communication

Best Practices

  1. Prepare and practice incident response
  1. Define clear roles and responsibilities
  1. Establish communication protocols
  1. Document everything
  1. Regular status updates
  1. Post-incident review and improvement
