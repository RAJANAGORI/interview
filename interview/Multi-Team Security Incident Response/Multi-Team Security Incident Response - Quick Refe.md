# Multi-Team Security Incident Response - Quick Reference

## **Incident Command Structure**

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **Incident Commander** | Overall coordination and decisions |
| **Technical Lead** | Technical decisions and guidance |
| **Communication Lead** | Stakeholder communication |
| **Legal/Compliance Lead** | Legal and compliance matters |
| **Business Lead** | Business impact and priorities |

## **Team Roles**

| Team | Key Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| **Security** | Threat analysis, containment guidance, forensics |
| **Engineering** | Code review, vulnerability fixes, deployment |
| **Operations** | System access, monitoring, infrastructure |
| **Legal** | Compliance, notifications, contracts |
| **PR** | Customer communication, public statements |

## **Communication Checklist**

- ✅ Incident response channel (Slack, Teams)
- ✅ Regular status updates (every 1-2 hours)
- ✅ Separate channels for technical vs. business
- ✅ Executive briefings
- ✅ Customer communication (if required)
- ✅ Document everything

## **Decision Authority**

| Level | Decisions | Authority |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tactical** | Technical decisions | Incident Commander |
| **Operational** | Business impact decisions | Business Lead |
| **Strategic** | High-impact decisions | Executive Leadership |

## **Communication Principles**

- Regular updates (even with incomplete information)
- Clear and concise
- Accurate information
- Don't speculate
- Appropriate detail level for audience

## **Key Principles**

- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Structured communication
- Data-driven decisions
- Collaborative problem-solving
- Post-incident learning
